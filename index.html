Understanding the Types of Aid
🎁
Grants
Free money based on financial need. You don’t repay a grant as long as you meet enrollment requirements. The Pell Grant and College Access Program Grant are the most common.
🏆
Scholarships
Free money awarded based on merit, field of study, background, or other criteria. KEES is Kentucky’s most well-known scholarship. Some scholarships require a service commitment — read the fine print.
💳
Loans
Money you borrow and must repay with interest. Federal loans generally have lower interest rates and more repayment options than private loans. Borrow only what you need.
🔄
Loan Repayment
Programs that pay off your existing student loans in exchange for working in a high-need field or location — common in health care, law, teaching, and public service. You must already have loans to qualify.
💰
Tax Credits
Reduce the amount of federal income tax you owe. The American Opportunity Credit (up to $2,500) and Lifetime Learning Credit (up to $2,000) are claimed when you file your tax return — not applied to tuition directly.
